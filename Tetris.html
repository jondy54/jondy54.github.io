Tetris is a puzzle video game originally designed by Alexey Pajitnov, Soviet Russian game designer, with the first playable version being made on June 6,1984. It was originally designed as a means to test the capabilities of new hardware at the Soviet Academy of Sciences in Moscow.
The main gameplay mechanics is that you in control of a tetromino, which is a geometric shape of four squares connected at right angles to each other. You are able to rotate the tetromino before it reaches the bottom of the screen and locks into place. Everytime a horizontal line is made on the screen, it will clear the line from the screen. The act of clearing four lines with one tetromino is what is known as a "Tetris". The objective of the game is to earn as many points as you can by clearing lines before your stack of tetrominoes reaches the top of the screen.
